Move towards secure attachment

When we get all of our needs met as a child, we move through the world with wonder, confidence, self-love, and a deep connection to who we are and our purpose. When we don't (which is true for so many of us), we carry the wounds of insecurity, fear, self-criticism, low self-esteem, and low self-confidence. It is possible to meet the needs that didn't get met and move into secure attachment.

Ideal parent figure (IPF) is a therapy methodology that meets our childhood needs. A pilot study found that four 16-minute IPF visualizations each week for a month significantly reduced symptoms of people with complex PTSD.

This program is designed to be a lot more than four 16-minute visualizations carried out over a month. We want to help you experience what you didn't think was possible (or did not even know you were missing), and help your nervous system learn a felt sense of security, safety, comfort, attunement, delight in your being, and support for exploration.

This is a first pilot, built on what I've learned from all of my training, coaching clients, and running Ideal Mother & Father workshops. It is heavily influenced by the Ideal Parent Figure methodology, but also expands on it, weaving in parts work, the divine masculine and feminine, and energetic work.

About me

Hi, I'm Amanda.

I've been on a journey into my inner world for the last 8 years. During that time, I've explored every modality I can find – from traditional therapies like CBT and psychoanalysis, to somatic modalities like focusing and parts work, to awakening and energy healing.

Doing ideal parent figure was one of the first times I realized what it could feel like to get my needs met.

I've been doing this therapy for 8 months. In that time, I've had many moments of deep, felt senses of relief. I've tasted what it's like to actually have unconditional love and a true belief that I am safe and cared for. I feel like Mary in the colorless room: Before, I could describe what security would feel like and point to moments where I had felt more secure. But what I've felt since starting IPF is like a different qualia that I couldn't have imagined before. I didn't possess the experiential building blocks to be able to imagine this feeling of security.

I now coach people with a combination of ideal parent figure, parts work, and energy healing. Ideal parent figure has been so helpful for my clients, and I want to share that experience with more people.
